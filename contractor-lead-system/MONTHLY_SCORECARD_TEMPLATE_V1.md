# Contractor Lead Conversion System — Monthly Scorecard Template V1

## Objective
Define the monthly report format a client receives to show the value of the service.

This report should help answer:
- how many leads came in
- how many missed calls were recovered
- how many leads were qualified
- how many estimates were booked
- where leads came from
- where money may still be leaking

The scorecard is meant to justify the monthly retainer.

---

# 1. Scorecard Principles
The monthly scorecard should be:
- short
- easy to scan
- money-focused
- honest
- useful
- not overloaded with vanity metrics

Avoid:
- meaningless charts
- fake ROI math
- inflated claims
- pages of data nobody reads

---

# 2. Top-Level Monthly Summary
## Header
- Client name
- Trade
- Reporting period
- Prepared date

## Executive summary block
A short paragraph:

"This month, your lead recovery system handled {{total_leads}} inbound leads, recovered {{recovered_missed_calls}} missed-call opportunities, qualified {{qualified_leads}} leads, and helped drive {{booked_estimates}} booked estimates. The main focus next month is {{top_optimization_focus}}."

---

# 3. Core KPI Section
Show these first:
- Total inbound leads
- Missed calls
- Recovered missed calls
- Recovery rate
- Qualified leads
- Qualification rate
- Booking requests
- Booked estimates
- Average response time
- Manual follow-up required

## KPI definitions
### Total inbound leads
All calls/forms/texts that entered the system and were treated as lead opportunities.

### Missed calls
Calls not answered live by the business.

### Recovered missed calls
Missed calls where the lead engaged after automated follow-up.

### Recovery rate
Recovered missed calls / total missed calls

### Qualified leads
Leads that match service area, service type, and buyer intent.

### Qualification rate
Qualified leads / total leads

### Booking requests
Leads who reached the booking or estimate-request step.

### Booked estimates
Appointments confirmed or clearly scheduled.

### Average response time
Average time from missed lead event to first response.

### Manual follow-up required
Leads that needed human callback/intervention.

- Leads In
- Contacted
- Qualified

# 6. Missed-Call Recovery Section
Show:
- total missed calls
- number recovered
- number unrecovered
- top recovery insight

## Example commentary
"You had 41 missed calls this month. 18 of those were recovered through text follow-up, which means 43.9% of missed opportunities were re-engaged instead of going cold immediately."

# 7. Lead Quality Section
Show:
- qualified leads
- unqualified leads
- junk/spam/vendor volume
- out-of-area volume
- unsupported-service volume

Purpose:
Help client see whether poor lead quality is a source problem or a handling problem.

# 11. Optimization Notes
This is the most important human section.

Each month include:
- what improved
- where leads were lost
- what should be adjusted next

## Template
### What worked
- Example: after-hours recovery improved
- Example: roofing estimate requests converted well

### What leaked
- Example: many leads asked for callback instead of booking
- Example: out-of-area traffic was high

### What we’ll improve next
- Example: tighten service-area filtering
- Example: revise first follow-up timing
- Example: adjust qualification question order

---

# 12. ROI Framing Rules
Do not invent revenue.
Do not make fake close-rate claims.

Allowed:
- show booked estimates
- show recovered leads
- show qualified leads
- show missed-call recovery count
- show conversion movement

Only include dollar estimates if the client has provided verified average job value or close-rate assumptions.

## Safe ROI example
"18 missed calls were recovered this month. 7 of those became qualified leads and 3 reached booked estimate status."

## Unsafe ROI example
"We made you $48,700 this month" unless supported by verified closed-job data.

---

# 13. Recommended Report Format
## V1 report layout
1. Header + executive summary
2. KPI snapshot
3. Source breakdown
4. Funnel / pipeline breakdown
5. Missed-call recovery performance
6. Booking / estimate section
7. Optimization notes
8. Next month focus

Keep it to one screen or one short PDF-style page if possible.

# 15. Suggested Client-Facing Language
Use language like:
- leads handled
- missed calls recovered
- qualified opportunities
- booked estimates
- average response time
- follow-up coverage

Avoid language like:
- AI orchestration layer
- autonomous agents
- neural automation
- anything hypey

---

# 16. Monthly Delivery Process
Each month:
1. pull metrics
2. review anomalies
3. write short human summary
4. identify 1–3 improvements
5. deliver scorecard to client

This turns reporting into retention.
