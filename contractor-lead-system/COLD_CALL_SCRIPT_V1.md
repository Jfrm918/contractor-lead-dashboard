# Contractor Lead Conversion System — Cold Call Script V1

## Objective
Give Jason's wife a simple script to start outreach to contractor businesses.

The goal is not to sound perfect.
The goal is to:
- get attention
- identify pain
- create curiosity
- book the next conversation

---

# 1. Core Rule
Do **not** try to explain the whole system on the first call.

The purpose of the first cold call is:
- start a conversation
- identify whether they buy leads / miss calls / have slow follow-up issues
- see if there is pain
- book a follow-up or demo

---

# 2. Best Tone
Use:
- calm
- direct
- local-business normal
- confident
- brief

Do NOT sound:
- scripted in a robotic way
- overexcited
- pushy
- “AI agency” weird
- too technical

---

# 3. Best Opening Script
## Gatekeeper / whoever answers
"Hey, quick question — who handles missed calls or inbound leads for the company?"

If transferred, continue.

If the owner/manager answers directly:

"Hey, this is [NAME]. I’ll be quick. We help contractors recover missed inbound leads and respond faster when calls get missed or come in after hours. I was just calling to see how you’re handling that right now."

That is the best opener.

---

# 4. If They Ask “What Is This About?”
"We help contractors convert more of the leads they already pay for by automatically following up on missed calls, qualifying prospects, and helping push more of them toward booking."

Short and clean.

---

# 5. Discovery Questions
Only ask 1–2 at first.

## Best questions
- "Are you guys already running Google Ads or buying leads right now?"
- "What usually happens if a call gets missed?"
- "Do after-hours leads usually get handled right away or the next day?"
- "Do you feel like some paid leads are slipping through the cracks?"

Goal:
find pain, not interrogate them.

---

# 6. Simple Value Pitch
If they show interest:

"That’s exactly the problem we help with. Instead of losing that lead, the system texts back fast, qualifies the person, and flags hot leads so your team can follow up faster."

Optional add-on:

"It sits on top of what you already do, so it doesn’t require ripping out your current setup."

That reduces resistance.

---

# 7. Main CTA
Do not hard-close a cold call first.
Book the next step.

## Best CTA
"Would you be open to a quick 10-minute walkthrough sometime this week so you can see what it looks like?"

Alternative:
"If I sent you a quick overview and demo link, would you take a look?"

Best recommendation:
**book the walkthrough if possible**

---

# 8. If They Say “We Already Have Someone For Marketing”
"That makes sense — this actually isn’t about replacing marketing. It’s more about making sure the leads you’re already paying for don’t get wasted when calls get missed or follow-up is slow."

This is important.

---

# 9. If They Say “We Don’t Miss Calls”
"That’s good. Most companies still have after-hours calls, busy periods, or leads that don’t get followed up on instantly. That’s usually where this helps."

Do not argue.

---

# 10. If They Say “Send Me Something”
"Absolutely. What’s the best email for you?"

Then say:
"I’ll send a short overview and demo link so you can see exactly what it looks like."

This means you need email follow-up ready.

---

# 11. If They Say “How Much Is It?”
"It depends a little on setup and how you want it configured, but for most companies it’s a simple monthly service around missed-call recovery, qualification, and lead handling. If it makes sense after a quick look, I can break pricing down clearly."

Do not get dragged into a full pricing debate cold unless needed.

---

# 12. If They Say “Not Interested”
Use a soft final line:

"No problem — quick last question before I let you go: are you already confident no paid leads are being lost from missed calls or slow follow-up?"

Sometimes that reopens the conversation.

If still no:
move on.

---

# 13. If They Say “Call Me Back Later”
"Sure — what’s better for you, later this afternoon or tomorrow?"

Try to pin down a time.

---

# 14. Voicemail Script
"Hey, this is [NAME]. We help contractors recover missed inbound leads and respond faster when calls get missed or come in after hours. I wanted to see if that’s something worth a quick conversation on your end. My number is [NUMBER]. Again, [NUMBER]."

Keep it short.

---

# 15. Best Short Script Version
If she wants the shortest usable version:

"Hey, this is [NAME]. I’ll be quick. We help contractors recover missed inbound leads and follow up faster when calls get missed or come in after hours. I was just curious how you’re handling that right now."

Then listen.

---

# 16. Call Goal by Outcome
## Bad outcome
- long awkward pitch
- no question asked
- no next step

## Good outcome
One of these happened:
- pain identified
- email collected
- demo booked
- callback time set

That is enough.

---

# 17. Best Niches to Call First
Start with:
- HVAC
- plumbing
- roofing
- electrical
- insulation
- garage doors

Why:
- they live on inbound calls
- lead value is meaningful
- missed calls are expensive

---

# 18. Recommended Offer Language on Calls
Use this exact framing:

"We help contractors convert more of the leads they already pay for by instantly following up on missed calls, qualifying prospects, and helping book more estimates."

That is the cleanest version.

---

# 19. Best Practice for Jason’s Wife
She does not need to sound like a closer.
She needs to sound:
- normal
- sharp
- brief
- consistent

Her job is to:
- open the conversation
- identify pain
- get the next step

That is it.
