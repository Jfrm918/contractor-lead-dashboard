# Contractor Lead Conversion System — Objection Handling Sheet V1

## Objective
Give Jason and his wife clean, short answers to common sales objections.

Rule:
- answer directly
- do not get defensive
- do not over-explain
- move back to the business problem

---

## 1. “We already have someone doing marketing.”
"That makes sense. This actually isn’t about replacing marketing — it’s about making sure the leads you’re already paying for don’t get wasted when calls get missed or follow-up is slow."

---

## 2. “We don’t miss calls.”
"That’s good. Most companies still have after-hours calls, busy periods, or leads that don’t get handled instantly. That’s usually where this helps."

---

## 3. “We already text people back.”
"That’s a good start. The difference here is making it consistent, trackable, and visible so missed opportunities don’t depend on someone remembering to follow up every time."

---

## 4. “We’re too busy right now.”
"That’s usually exactly when missed leads become more expensive. The setup is meant to be simple and sit on top of what you already do."

---

## 5. “How much is it?”
"The pilot is simple — $500 setup and $500 a month. That covers the missed-call recovery flow, qualification setup, alerts, dashboard access, and monthly reporting."

---

## 6. “That sounds expensive.”
"I get that. The way to look at it is whether missed calls and slow follow-up are already costing more than that. For companies already paying for leads, it usually doesn’t take many recovered opportunities for the system to make sense."

---

## 7. “We already have a CRM.”
"That’s fine. This isn’t about ripping out your CRM — it’s a conversion layer on top of your current setup to help with missed calls, follow-up, and lead visibility."

---

## 8. “We don’t want more software.”
"Totally fair. This is meant to reduce missed opportunities, not create more complexity. We keep the setup simple and make it easy to add without overhauling your process."

---

## 9. “Send me something.”
"Absolutely. I can send a short overview and the demo so you can see exactly what it looks like. What’s the best email for you?"

---

## 10. “We need to think about it.”
"Of course. Usually the real question is whether missed calls and slow follow-up are costing you enough that it’s worth fixing now. If it helps, I can send the pilot details and next steps so you can look at it clearly."

---

## 11. “We handle that in-house.”
"That’s fine. This usually helps businesses that want their in-house team to have better follow-up coverage, alerts, and visibility without relying on manual consistency every time."

---

## 12. “We only want leads, not more systems.”
"I understand. The issue is that more leads don’t help much if some of the ones you already have are slipping through the cracks. This is built to improve conversion from the leads already coming in."

---

## 13. “Call me later.”
"Sure — what’s better for you, later today or tomorrow?"

---

## 14. “Not interested.”
"No problem. Quick question before I let you go — are you fully confident no paid leads are being lost from missed calls or slow follow-up?"

---

## 15. Best practice
Do not try to win every argument.
Your job is to:
- lower resistance
- reframe the problem
- move to the next step
