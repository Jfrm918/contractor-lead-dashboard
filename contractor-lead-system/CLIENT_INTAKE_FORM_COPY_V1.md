# Contractor Lead Conversion System — Client Intake Form Copy V1

## Objective
Provide the exact client-facing copy and questions for the intake form.

---

# Intro Copy
Thanks for getting started.

This form gives us the information we need to set up your lead recovery system so missed calls and inbound leads can be followed up on faster and more consistently.

Please keep answers as clear and simple as possible.

---

# Section 1 — Company Basics
## Fields
- Company name
- Primary contact name
- Best contact email
- Best contact mobile
- Website
- Trade / business type
- City / state

---

# Section 2 — Phone and Alerts
## Fields
- Main business phone line
- Best forwarding number
- After-hours number if different
- Best mobile number for hot lead alerts
- Best email for lead alerts

---

# Section 3 — Service Area
## Fields
- What cities do you serve?
- Any ZIP codes you want us to know?
- Are there any areas you do not serve?
- Do emergency-service rules differ by area?

---

# Section 4 — Services Offered
## Fields
- What services do you want leads qualified for?
- What services do you NOT offer?
- Do you offer emergency service?
- Any minimum project/job size we should know about?

---

# Section 5 — Scheduling and Booking
## Fields
- Do you use a booking link right now?
- If yes, what is the booking URL?
- If no, who handles callbacks or scheduling?
- What is the preferred callback window?

---

# Section 6 — Lead Rules
## Fields
- What questions do you want asked first?
- What makes a lead a good fit?
- What should be treated as junk or disqualified?
- What should be treated as urgent?
- What should trigger an immediate owner alert?

---

# Section 7 — Messaging Preferences
## Fields
- What business name should be used in text messages?
- Preferred tone: direct, friendly, premium, other
- Any words or phrases we should avoid?
- Anything important we should know before texting leads on your behalf?

---

# Final Confirmation Copy
Once we have this, we’ll set up the system, test it, and confirm launch readiness before anything goes live.
